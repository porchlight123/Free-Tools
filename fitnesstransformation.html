Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this fitness calculator?

Our calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, considered the gold standard for metabolic rate calculation. However, individual results may vary by 10-15% based on genetics, hormones, and lifestyle factors. Use it as a starting point and adjust based on your progress.

Should I eat back exercise calories?

Our calculator already factors in your activity level, so you don't need to add exercise calories. If you're consistently losing weight too quickly (more than 2 lbs/week), consider increasing calories by 100-200 per day.

How often should I recalculate?

Recalculate every 10-15 pounds of weight change, or if your activity level significantly changes. As you lose weight, your caloric needs decrease, so regular adjustments ensure continued progress.

What if I'm not seeing results?

First, ensure you're accurately tracking your food intake. If you're certain about your tracking, try reducing calories by 100-150 per day. Also consider factors like sleep, stress, and water retention which can mask fat loss.

Can I build muscle while losing fat?

Yes, especially if you're new to training or returning after a break. Focus on progressive overload in the gym, maintain high protein intake (1g per lb bodyweight), and don't cut calories too aggressively.

How important is meal timing?

Total daily intake matters most. However, eating protein every 3-5 hours can optimize muscle protein synthesis. Pre and post-workout nutrition can also enhance performance and recovery.
